By Zane Al-HamwyJanuary 2025

NLP-Powered Chatbot

Participating in QHacks and developing Lease Me Before You Go Go was an exciting and transformative experience that challenged me to apply my technical skills in a real-world scenario. As a backend developer, I built the project using Python, Flask, and SpaCy, leveraging natural language processing (NLP) to create a chatbot that simulated interactions between students and landlords. The goal was to help students prepare for renting by allowing them to engage in realistic conversations, ensuring they knew what to expect and what important questions to ask. The chatbot analyzed user inputs and provided real-time feedback, while the grading system, powered by cosine similarity in SpaCy, evaluated responses based on the number of high-quality questions asked. By the end of the interaction, users received a score reflecting how well they navigated the conversation, helping them improve their knowledge and confidence in dealing with real landlords. This project pushed me to think critically about user experience, backend development, and NLP implementation, while also strengthening my problem-solving skills under the fast-paced, high-pressure environment of a hackathon. Seeing our idea come to life and function as intended was an incredibly rewarding moment, solidifying my passion for AI-driven applications and software development.
